# h2a-visa-agricultural-labor-analysis
This group research evaluates the H2A Temporary Agricultural Workers program as a critical component of the U.S. agricultural supply chain. By analyzing labor demand and federal wage requirements, this project explores the economic trade-offs producers face between rising labor costs and the necessity of a stable, "boots-on-the-ground" workforce for specialty crop production.

Core Analysis & Methodology
AEWR Analysis: Investigated trends in the Adverse Effect Wage Rate (AEWR) and its direct pressure on farm-level margins.

Labor Market Reliance: Analyzed the shift from domestic labor to guest worker programs in Florida and the Southeast.

Operational Logistics: Evaluated the hidden costs of H2A compliance, including housing, transportation, and administrative oversight.

Commodity Impact: Studied the correlation between labor availability and harvest efficiency for soft commodities and specialty crops..
